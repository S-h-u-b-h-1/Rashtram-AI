# Demo Walkthrough

Target duration: 9 minutes

## 0:00–0:45 — Positioning

Open the landing page. Explain that Rashtram AI is an evidence-first Indian
legislative and public-policy research workspace. Emphasize retained source
links and that the official record remains authoritative.

## 0:45–1:45 — Authentication and dashboard

Sign in and open Dashboard. Show the daily brief, global search, quick actions,
last verified refresh, grouped legislative updates, recent research,
high-confidence recommendations when present, trending ministries, policy
updates, and compact source health. Explain that every visible item is a real
database record; empty sections are hidden rather than filled with examples.

## 1:45–2:35 — Parliament Bills and State Bills

Expand Bills in the sidebar. Briefly show Parliament Bills, then State Bills.
Filter State Bills by State and Year, change sorting, and open one result.
Point out that missing source status is not converted into a synthetic label.

## 2:35–3:20 — Parliament Acts and State Acts

Expand Acts and move between Parliament Acts and State Acts. Show that both
libraries use the same filters, pagination, comparison selection, and research
workspace while retaining their jurisdiction boundary.

## 3:20–4:20 — Policies

Open Policies. Move across National Policies, State Policies, Schemes,
Guidelines, Office Memoranda, Consultation Papers, Circulars, Framework
Documents, and Reports & White Papers. Use Ministry, jurisdiction, year,
source, and PDF filters. Open a strong official record.

## 4:20–6:10 — Document research

In the three-pane workspace, read the original PDF on the left, inspect the AI
brief and metadata in the middle, and use research chat on the right. Show
timeline, relationships, recommendations, source, bookmark, notes, and export.
Ask one specific question and show citations to original-language passages.
Mention automatic OCR for scanned Hindi documents and the visible retry state
when processing cannot complete.

## 6:10–6:55 — Cross-document analysis

Return to a catalogue, select two to five records, and open Compare in one chat.
Ask for a difference grounded in the selected documents.

## 6:55–7:40 — Research history and profile

Open Profile. Show editable identity and research preferences, preferred
states, saved content, bookmarks, collections, reading history, data consent,
export, and active sessions.

## 7:40–8:30 — Global search

Open Search Everything with Command-K. Search across legislation, policies,
Gazette records, committee reports, rules, and notifications. Show recent
research and saved searches, then open a result directly.

## 8:30–9:00 — Close

Return to the dashboard and summarize: real public records, consistent
research workflow, transparent source health, and grounded AI assistance.
