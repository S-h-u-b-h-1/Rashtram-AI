# Demo Walkthrough

Target duration: 8 minutes

## 0:00–0:45 — Positioning

Open the landing page. Explain that Rashtram AI is an evidence-first Indian
legislative and public-policy research workspace. Emphasize retained source
links and that the official record remains authoritative.

## 0:45–2:00 — Dashboard

Sign in and open Dashboard. Show the daily brief, last refresh, global search,
recent activity, recent research, policy reading, recent State Bills, and
compact source health. If useful, open `/app?demo=1` and explain that every
featured item is a real database record.

## 2:00–3:10 — State Bills

Open State Bills. Filter by State and Year, change sorting, and open one result.
Point out the explicit “status not reported” behavior where the source omits
status. Open its official PDF or source.

## 3:10–4:20 — Policies

Open Policies. Show Ministry, State/jurisdiction, category, year, source, and
PDF filters. Open a Ministry of Environment guideline and its official PDF.

## 4:20–6:00 — Document research

In a document workspace, show metadata, summary availability, timeline,
relationships, related documents, source, PDF, bookmark, and notes. Ask one
specific question. Show streamed Markdown and citations to indexed passages.
State clearly that unsupported points are not inferred.

## 6:00–6:50 — Cross-document analysis

Return to a catalogue, select two to five records, and open Compare in one chat.
Ask for a difference grounded in the selected documents.

## 6:50–7:35 — Profile

Open Profile. Show editable identity and research preferences, preferred
states, saved content, bookmarks, collections, reading history, data consent,
export, and active sessions.

## 7:35–8:00 — Close

Return to the dashboard and summarize: real public records, consistent
research workflow, transparent source health, and grounded AI assistance.
